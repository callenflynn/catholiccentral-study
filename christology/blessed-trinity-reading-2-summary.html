Blessed Trinity Reading 2 - Summary

1. Immaculate Conception

  • Mary was conceived without Original Sin.
  • God gave her a special grace so she could be the perfect Mother of Jesus.
  • This was done in anticipation of Christ's merits (Jesus saved her before she ever sinned).
  • Officially declared a dogma in 1854 by Pope Pius IX (Ineffabilis Deus).
  • Mary confirmed it in Lourdes when she said: "I am the Immaculate Conception."
  • Because she had no Original Sin:
    • She had no concupiscence (no disordered desires).
    • She remained sinless her entire life.

2. Mary Ever-Virgin

  • The Church teaches Mary was a virgin:
    • Before, during, and after the birth of Jesus.
    • This is called Perpetual Virginity.
  • Jesus was conceived by the Holy Spirit, not a human father.
  • Church Fathers + councils (like Lateran Council, 649) taught this.
  • The "brothers and sisters of Jesus" in the Bible were cousins or relatives (Aramaic had no word for cousin).
  • Jesus giving Mary to John at the Cross proves she had no other children.
  • Her virginity shows total dedication to God and the value of a life given fully to Him.

3. Our Lady of Lourdes

  • Mary appeared to St. Bernadette in 1858 in Lourdes, France.
  • Bernadette was a poor 14-year-old.
  • Mary asked for:
    • Prayer for sinners
    • Bernadette to dig â†’ a miraculous spring appeared.
  • On March 25, Mary said: "I am the Immaculate Conception."
  • There were 18 apparitions.
  • The Church approved them in 1862.
  • 67 officially confirmed miracles have occurred at Lourdes.

4. The Assumption

  • Mary was taken into Heaven body and soul at the end of her earthly life.
  • Celebrated on August 15.
  • Defined as dogma in 1950 by Pope Pius XII (Munificentissimus Deus).
  • Similar things happened to:
    • Enoch
    • Elijah
    • Moses
  • Mary is uniquely close to God:
    • Daughter of the Father
    • Mother of the Son
    • Spouse of the Holy Spirit
  • Revelation's "woman clothed with the sun" points to Mary.
  • Mary is the Ark of the New Covenant because she carried Jesus.
