Great circle: Circles passing through Earth's center

Small circle: All other circles

Describe which map projections are used for what purposes.

Line with 0 degrees longitude

Describe the purposes served by sectional aeronautical charts.

  • Charts that show terrain and features
  • Has control tower = blue
  • No control tower = magenta

Name methods of showing relief on a sectional aeronautical chart.

  • Color tints, contour lines, highest points, and pictorial relief

Identify symbols used to indicate cultural features on a sectional aeronautical chart.

  • Roads, railroads, bridges, cities/towns, borders/boundaries

Explain why hydrographic features are a valuable navigational aid.

  • They provide easily recognizable visual landmarks that help pilots confirm their position and direction.

Name the two broadest classifications of airports.

  • Towered
  • nontowered

Describe an airport using the symbols and data printed on a sectional aeronautical chart.

 Define joint-use airports.

  • Used by civilian and military aircraft

Name the agency responsible for regulating planes, pilots, and airspace.

  • FAA

Describe controlled airspace and its subdivisions.

  • Controlled airspace is airspace where ATC provides services. Subdivisions:
  • A: 18,000–60,000 ft, IFR only
  • B: Busy airports, clearance required
  • C: Medium airports, two-way radio required
  • D: Small towered airports, two-way radio required
  • E: Other controlled airspace, IFR & VFR allowed

Identify special-use airspace categories.

  • Prohibited – no entry
  • Restricted – permission needed
  • Warning – hazardous activities
  • MOA – military training
  • Alert – high activity
  • CFA – firing stops if aircraft nearby

 Describe the functions of specialized aeronautical charts.

  • help pilots navigate, avoid hazards, and comply with airspace rules.

 Identify the major factors influencing air navigation.

  • weather, airspace, terrain, aircraft performance, navigation aids, and traffic.

State two causes of magnetic variation.

  • Difference between true north and magnetic north
  • Earths magnetic field

Explain why compass deviation occurs.

  • caused by nearby magnetic interference.

 Describe the purpose of a wind triangle.

  • shows how wind affects an aircraft’s heading and ground speed.

Define true airspeed and ground speed.

  • True Airspeed: Speed through the air.
  • Ground Speed: Speed over the ground.

Explain pilotage navigation.

  • Pilotage is navigating by sighting landmarks.

 Describe dead-reckoning navigation.

  • navigating using speed, time, and direction.

 Identify the basic steps involved in dead-reckoning navigation.

  • Set course
  • Measure speed
  • Track time
  • Estimate position

Describe the use of the aircraft radio as a navigational aid.

  • help pilots navigate by receiving signals from ground beacons.

Explain the use of the VOR/TACAN receiver as a navigational aid.

  • shows a plane’s direction and distance from a ground station.

Identify limitations of the automatic direction finder (ADF) as a navigational aid.

  • ADF can be affected by weather, terrain, and signal range, causing errors.

 Describe the use of distance-measuring equipment (DME) as a navigational aid.

  • shows the aircraft’s distance from a ground station.

 Name the parts of the VOR System for navigation.

  • ground station
  • aircraft receiver
  • cockpit indicator.

 Describe how to navigate using the VOR System

  •  tune to the station, identify it, select a radial, and follow the needle.

 Describe how to plot a position on a LORAN chart.

  • plot your position at the intersection of time-difference lines from stations.

Name the two types of GPS positioning systems and who might use each.

  • Civilian GPS: for public and commercial use
  • Military GPS: for armed forces and secure navigation

 Describe why the Inertial Navigation System is different than the other systems.

  • navigates using onboard sensors, without external signals.
